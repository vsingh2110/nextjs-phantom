# Content Strategy & Information Design

- **Project:** MindScape Research (working title)
- **Updated:** 29 October 2025

## Editorial Objectives
- Provide evidence-backed insights on social and cyber psychology, digital propaganda, and narrative manipulation.
- Balance academic rigor with approachable summaries for broader audiences.
- Offer tiered depth: public blog summaries → detailed research papers → premium reports and courses.

## Content Types
1. **Research Articles**
   - Long-form analyses (~2,500–5,000 words) with data visualization.
   - Include methodology section, references, downloadable PDF.
   - Public access by default; flag subset as premium when appropriate.
2. **Blog Posts**
   - Timely commentary, trend analyses, interviews.
   - 800–1,500 words, accessible language, strong SEO orientation.
3. **Reports**
   - Premium deep dives, proprietary datasets, actionable recommendations.
   - Available to paid members or one-off purchasers.
4. **Courses & Workshops**
   - Video/modules, quizzes, transcripts, resource downloads.
   - Positioned for practitioners seeking applied knowledge.
5. **Newsletters**
   - Weekly digest summarizing new content, research highlights, calls-to-action.
   - Encourage conversions to membership.

## Publishing Cadence (Initial Target)
- Research articles: 2 per month.
- Blog posts: 1–2 per week.
- Premium reports: 1 per quarter (scale up as capacity grows).
- Courses: Launch first flagship course by end of Phase 3.
- Newsletter: Weekly send on Tuesdays.

## Taxonomy & Metadata
- **Domains:** Social Psychology, Cyber Psychology, Media Literacy, Policy, Technology, Case Studies.
- **Personas:** Policy Maker, Journalist, Academic, Educator, Practitioner.
- **Tags:** Use consistent, documented list; avoid duplicates (e.g., "social-media" vs "social media").
- **SEO Fields:** Title, meta description, canonical URL, Open Graph image per entry.

## Content Workflow
1. Ideation documented in shared tracker with summary, target persona, keywords.
2. Draft creation in Supabase or MDX; assign author and editor.
3. Editorial review for accuracy, clarity, bias mitigation.
4. Fact-checking and citation verification.
5. Design/visualization pass for charts or infographics.
6. Final approval by publisher/admin, schedule release, share to newsletter pipeline.

## Style & Voice Guidelines (Draft)
- Use active voice, plain language, avoid jargon without explanation.
- Cite sources using APA or MLA consistently; link open-access references.
- Maintain neutral tone; acknowledge uncertainties and research limitations.
- Emphasize ethical considerations and transparency.

## Accessibility Considerations
- Provide alt text for all images and ensure color contrast ratios.
- Offer downloadable transcripts for audio/video content.
- Use semantic HTML headings to support screen readers.

## Analytics & Measurement
- Track page views, engagement time, scroll depth, conversion events (newsletter signup, membership purchase).
- Tag campaigns to attribute traffic sources (UTM parameters).
- Review KPIs bi-weekly during MVP, adjust strategy accordingly.
